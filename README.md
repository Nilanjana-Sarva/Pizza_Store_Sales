# Pizza_Store_Sales

 **Key Performance Indicators**:
 These metrics represent the financial and operational health of the pizza business.
 
 Total Revenue: 817,860.05 (The total income generated from all pizza sales).
 
 Total Pizzas Sold: 49,574 (The volume of product moved through the kitchen).
 
 Total Orders: 21,350 (Total number of transactions processed).
 
 Average Order Value (AOV): 38.31 (The average amount spent per transaction).
 
 Average Pizzas Per Order: 2.32 (Typical basket size, indicating high potential for multi-item orders).
 
 Top Revenue Category: Classic Pizzas (Contributing 220,053.10 to total sales).2. 
 
**Business Objectives**:
Based on the operational flow, the strategic focus areas are:

Revenue Optimization: Maximizing the contribution of high-value pizzas like the Thai Chicken Pizza.

Menu Engineering: Identifying "underperformers" (like the Brie Carre Pizza) for potential removal or redesign to save on specialized inventory costs.

Operational Efficiency: Aligning staffing and inventory with Peak Hours (Lunch 12–1 PM and Dinner 5–7 PM).

Average Ticket Growth: Encouraging customers to move from "Small" or "Medium" sizes to "Large," which currently accounts for nearly 46% of total revenue.

**Key Findings & Strategic Insights**

Product & Category PerformanceRevenue Leaders: The Chicken category yields the highest revenue per individual pizza type (Thai Chicken, Barbecue Chicken, California Chicken).

The Size Factor: Large (L) size pizzas are the primary revenue driver, generating 375,318 more than double the revenue of Small (S) pizzas.

Variety Efficiency: While "Classic" has the most sales by volume, the "Chicken" and "Supreme" categories have a higher price-per-unit contribution.

Operational Insights (Time & Attendance)Peak Period Clusters: Sales are heavily concentrated during two specific windows: 12:00 PM – 1:00 PM (Lunch rush) and 5:00 PM – 7:00 PM (Dinner rush).

Seasonal Stability: Unlike the insurance data which showed long-term trends, pizza sales are relatively stable month-to-month, with slight peaks in July and May.

Revenue ConcentrationPareto Effect in Menu: A handful of "Top 5" pizzas generate a disproportionate share of the revenue. Conversely, the "Bottom 5" pizzas represent less than 80,000 combined, indicating potential "menu bloat."
